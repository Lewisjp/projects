"Louise and Maggie's wedding was beautiful." Bella said with a smile to Jeremy. "I'm happy she kept her pink hair." The couple had just got home from a fun weekend in Pennsylvania. They didn't expect what was waiting for them in their fish tank...

A Fat Betta fish. "I didn't even know they could get fat." Jeremy thought fasting was the answer. Bella found a different solution online, peas. According to articles, the fish was likely constipated. A cleanse consisting of a pea diet could help.

Water break….but don’t be going down the hall and getting a Crispy Cream. - Tony Horton



How to solve this first world problem:
  1. Boil peas till they are soft
  2. Remove pea skin
  3. Break up the inner pea parts into Betta fish bite sizes
  4. Feed soft pea parts to the constipated fish

Even on a pea diet, our lazy fat fish just sat around the castle. Sometimes you gotta get those fins moving. We bought a Gold Inca Snail to work out the Betta Fish from time to time.

You're probably wondering... How can I prevent my herbivorous fish from becoming constipated?

  • A varied diet
    • Greens, peas, cucumber etc
    • Pellets
    • Avoid Flakes
  • Feed fish 6 days a week
  • Don't over feeding it
